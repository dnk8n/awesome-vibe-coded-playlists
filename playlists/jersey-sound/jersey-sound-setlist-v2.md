## ★ The central track — re-evaluated per the authorities

**Intense – "Let The Rain Come Down" (Down Pour Mix) — Movin' Records MR008, February 1990.**
✅ [release/80280](https://www.discogs.com/release/80280) · [master/325086](https://www.discogs.com/master/325086) · A1, 7:48 · Credits verified on the pressing itself: **Mixed By – Tony Humphries · Executive-Producer – Abigail Adams** & Ace Mungin · Vocals – Jazmina & Khison.

Why this record, freshly interpreted against the information authorities on the Jersey Sound:

1. **The community's own #1.** The "Jersey Sound Top 100" (FIDA/ThinkSoul Committee, Dec 2019 — the scene's own 30-years-later reckoning) ranks it **first out of one hundred**.
2. **Humphries' own hand.** It is not merely a record he played: the Discogs credits put him inside the record ("Mixed By – Tony Humphries"). Licensed from Newark's Ace Beat to Movin', his remix is what turned it into the anthem.
3. **The scene's institutions in one artifact.** Movin' Records — Abigail Adams' East Orange shop/label, which Humphries himself called "the glue" of the scene — is the label *and* Adams is executive producer.
4. **The sound itself.** A gospel-phrased female vocal over deep, swinging house — the literal embodiment of the gospel→club throughline this whole setlist traces.
5. **The right moment.** February 1990: dead-center of the Zanzibar residency / KISS-FM Mastermix golden era, unlike any candidate from the later Shelter years.

*What changed:* the previous central pick, **Kerri Chandler – "Climax 1"** (Atmosphere E.P. Vol. 1, 1993), was chosen for lineage (Chandler was himself a Zanzibar resident). But held against the authorities, it doesn't carry list backing — Chandler has **no individual entry anywhere in the Top 100**, and Bill Brewster's Mixmag 20 includes him only via Three Generations. Chandler remains this document's spine (15 appearances) and keeps 1993 🥇; the crown moves to the record the scene itself crowned. Strongest alternative reading: **Adeva – "Respect"** (Top 100 #2, leads Brewster's list) — see 1988.

## The authorities

- **Tony Humphries himself** — his Discogs production/mix credits (Joubert Singers, Visual, Intense, Ceybil all carry verified "Mixed By – Tony Humphries" credits); his own compilations: *Choice – A Collection of Club Zanzibar Classics* ([Azuli, 2003](https://www.discogs.com/release/174974)) and *Running Back Mastermix: The Kiss FM Zanzibar Years* (June 19, 2026, from his own DAT archive) — the 2026 tracklist is used below as primary evidence of "what he still plays."
- **The "Jersey Sound Top 100"** ([myhouseradio.fm](https://www.myhouseradio.fm/2019/12/18/the-jersey-sound-top-100-tunes-30-years-later-and-the-music-lives-on/) / [full list](http://flowerzindattic.blogspot.com/2019/12/the-jersey-sound-top-100-30-years-later.html), FIDA/ThinkSoul Committee, Dec 2019). Reliable for *what matters*, **not for dates** — every date used below was independently re-verified against actual Discogs pressings (this pass caught three more wrong years the list had propagated).
- **Bill Brewster's Mixmag ["The 20 Best New Jersey House Records"](https://mixmag.net/feature/best-new-jersey-house-records)** (Feb 2019).
- **Kerri Chandler** (Zanzibar resident), **Abigail Adams / Movin' Records**, the RBMA Zanzibar oral history, and Discogs' collector community — as in v1.
- **Zanzibar timeline:** club opened 1979; Humphries residency 1982–early '90s; KISS-FM Mastermix show into 1994. So 1960–1981 = lead-up, 1982+ = the era proper.
- **Jersey Sound ≠ Jersey Club** (Brick Bandits / DJ Tameil, 2000s Newark, ~140 bpm) — excluded entirely.
- ⚠️ **Name-clash warning (carried from v1):** the `@tonyhumphriesmusicyt` YouTube channel is an unrelated hip-hop artist. Every Humphries item below is verified against Discogs credits or Zanzibar/KISS-FM context.

Methodology, verified this pass: year = Discogs year of the linked pressing; original pressings preferred; where a later mix *is* the Jersey Sound moment (De'Lacy '95, First Choice '83 exception discussed in place) the doc says so explicitly; BPM/Camelot deliberately excluded.
