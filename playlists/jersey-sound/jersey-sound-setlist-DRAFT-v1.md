## Establishing the authorities

- **Tony Humphries' own account** (Red Bull Music Academy lecture; RBMA's "Nightclubbing: Zanzibar" oral history with Danny Krivit, Winston Jones, Gerald T. Roney, Anané) — and his own Discogs production/mix credits, which turned out to be a richer vein than his interviews alone. Case in point:

  > **The Joubert Singers – "Stand On The Word" (1985).** You mentioned hearing Humphries talk about this one specifically, and it's a big deal: Discogs credits the mix directly to him, tags the release Gospel/Disco/Boogie, and the video/reissue history shows it was mixed by *both* Larry Levan and Humphries at different points — a literal audio bridge between Paradise Garage and Zanzibar. It's also a genuine gospel choir recording (Phyliss Joubert wrote and arranged it) that crossed into club culture, which is the exact "gospel roots into garage" throughline this whole document is tracing from Section 1 onward. It now anchors 1985, replacing Colonel Abrams — good as "Trapped" is, it's a record Humphries *played*; "Stand On The Word" is a record he *made*, and it comes with a direct personal connection for you on top of that. Strongest possible combination.

- **Kerri Chandler**, who was *also* a Zanzibar resident DJ, not just an artist Humphries played (Grey Area, AllMusic, Wikipedia, Grokipedia).
- **Abigail Adams**, owner of Movin' Records (East Orange), the shop/label Humphries himself called "the glue" of the scene — her own interviews (The Vinyl Factory, Bandcamp Daily, the "afterthegarage" blog).
- **Bill Brewster's Mixmag "20 Best New Jersey House Records"** (Feb 2019) — Brewster co-founded DJHistory.com and co-wrote *Last Night a DJ Saved My Life*; about as credentialed as dance music journalism gets. This became a primary cross-reference source this pass.
- **The "Jersey Sound Top 100"** (myhouseradio.fm, compiled by the FIDA/ThinkSoul Committee, Dec 2019) — a exhaustive, year-dated, 100-deep community ranking built specifically to mark "30 years later." This is the single most useful source I've found for pinning exact years across 1985–2001, and I cross-referenced every pick in that window against it this pass.
- **Academic/journalistic history**: Jasmine A. Henry's Rutgers dissertation on Newark club music, The FADER's Jersey Club oral history, NJ Urban News, Wikipedia.
- **Discogs' own collector community**: a "Deep House vs Garage House" thread that uses "New Jersey Sound" as the community's own name for the genre, and a separate thread where users reverse-engineered a full 1992 Humphries KISS-FM Mastermix tracklist against real Discogs release IDs.
- **Tony Humphries right now**: he personally curated *Running Back Mastermix: Kiss FM Zanzibar Years* (June 19, 2026) from his own DAT tapes, used below for 2026.

**Jersey Sound ≠ Jersey Club** (the 2000s Brick Bandits/DJ Tameil genre) — related lore, same city, different genre and generation, no Humphries connection. Excluded entirely.

**Zanzibar timeline:** club opened 1979 (Larry Patterson, Tee Scott resident first); Humphries' residency 1982–early 90s; KISS-FM mix show into 1994. So 1960–1981 = leadup, 1982+ = the era proper.
