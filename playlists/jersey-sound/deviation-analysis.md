# Jersey Sound: v2 (pre-skill) vs v3 (skill-built) — deviation analysis

**Protocol.** v3 was curated independently: the builder read only the
[hand-written verification brief](CLAUDE_CODE_HANDOFF.md) (v1-era), never the v1/v2 pick
tables, until v3's 199 slots were assembled and validated. Only then were
[v1](jersey-sound-setlist-DRAFT-v1.md) and [v2](jersey-sound-setlist-v2.md) opened and
this analysis written. v2: 78 tracks, one-per-year with contention podiums. v3: 3-per-year
mandate, 199 picks + 37-deep bench, full YouTube/ratings layer.

## 1 · Convergences — independent agreement is the strongest canon signal

Roughly **45 picks landed identically** in both documents, several down to the *same
Discogs pressing chosen independently* (Sly's *Dance To The Music* LP — release 27345972
in both; Serious Intention's Easy Street 12"; Phase II's Movin' original). The shared
spine: Aretha→Adeva "Respect" echo, Isleys on T-Neck, MFSB, Ten Percent, Instant Funk,
D-Train, Peech Boys, Visual, Joubert Singers, Colonel Abrams, Sybil, Ce Ce Rogers,
Jomanda, Phase II, Intense, **Climax 1 + Deep Inside (1993)**, Kim English, Kimara,
Underground Ministries, "Do You Remember House?", Sandcastles, Most Precious Love,
Bar A Thym, Son Of Raw, Hey Hey, *Computer Games*, Honeycomb Vol 1, *Watergate Files*,
Kaoz Theory KT001, *25 Years Of Madhouse*, *Spaces And Places*, *A Quiet Mind*,
Lost & Found. Two independent methods, one canon.

**Date corrections cross-validated.** Both runs independently caught **Adeva "Respect" =
1988** (chart year 1989 propagated by every list) and **Kimara "When Can Our Love Begin" =
1998**. v3 additionally caught *Kimara "Only You" = 1997* (the brief itself said 1998) and
*Jomanda "Got A Love For You"'s 1990 album appearance* — which v2 documents too, from the
other direction. The brief's warning that fan-list dates rot was validated by two
independent verifiers.

## 3 · Where v3 adds what v2 lacks

- **The New Jersey-native proto layer.** v2's 1960s–70s are canonical national soul
  (Motown/Philly) with *zero* NJ-native content. v3 carries The Shirelles (Passaic),
  Dionne Warwick (East Orange — answered by Sybil's 1989 cover, an echo v2 misses),
  Linda Jones (Newark deep-soul melisma — the most direct vocal ancestor of Jersey
  garage), The Manhattans on Newark's Carnival, the Parliaments (Plainfield), the
  Moments/Sylvia on Englewood's All Platinum, Gloria Gaynor (Newark), Kool & The Gang
  (Jersey City), James Cleveland on Newark's Savoy — the gospel root on the gospel label.
- **Tony Humphries' own "Master Mix Medley" (1982)** — his own record in the year he took
  the Zanzibar residency. Absent from v2.
- **Cultural Vibe "Ma Foom Bey" (Easy Street, 1986, Tony Humphries mix)** — arguably the
  proto-Jersey record; surprisingly absent from v2's table.
- **Full 3-per-year coverage** (199 picks vs 78) with 2020s depth from the Kaoz
  Theory/Honeycomb label scans ("Prayer" 2021, "Caged Bird" 2024, Brian Jackson w/ Josh
  Milan & Moodymann 2025/2026).
- **"Lusting U" placed, not just replaced.** v2 documented the 2020 Discogs gap and
  substituted; v3 additionally seats the track at its true Discogs anchor — *Organized
  Kaoz EP 3* (2023) — so the brief's white whale is IN the canon at its verifiable year.
- **The maintenance layer**: YouTube links from release-page videos (177/199, all
  API-validated live), 1–5 ratings, machine-readable `playlist_items.json` — v2 has no
  video/playlist dimension at all.

## 4 · The one open judgment: the central track

The handoff brief (which v3 followed) locks **Kerri Chandler "Climax 1"** as central.
v2 — with authority backing — moved the crown to **Intense "Let The Rain Come Down (Down
Pour Mix)"** (Movin' MR008, Feb 1990): Top 100 **#1**, "Mixed By – Tony Humphries" on the
pressing, Abigail Adams as executive producer — the scene's institutions in one artifact.
Both records hold their year-slots in v3 either way; the "central" designation is
editorial. **Resolved by the curator (2026-07-19): neither.** The canon centers the
*artist* — Tony Humphries himself — which dissolves the contention: v2's case for Intense
was always really a case about Humphries' hand on the record, and the brief's case for
Climax 1 was about his club's lineage. Centering the man makes both era-peaks in one
orbit, alongside his own 1982 *Master Mix Medley*, his 1996/2013/2019 productions, and
the 2026 archive release that bookends the document.

## 5 · Deliberate non-adoptions

- v2's 1960s Motown golds (Mary Wells, Supremes, Four Tops, My Girl) — kept v3's
  gospel/NJ-native set per the project's songbook-first-plus-NJ framework; v2's picks are
  canonical pop-soul but carry no Jersey or club-lineage specificity.
- Cher "Take Me Home" (1979) — genuinely Humphries-attested (his own *Choice* comp) and
  noted here for the record; v3's 1979 (Machine / McFadden & Whitehead / Kool & The Gang)
  holds the slot on songbook + NJ grounds. Bench-listed.
- v2's single-pick discipline — v3's 3-per-year mandate comes from the current brief.
