# Remix Rave — The Originals (Pre-'00 Sources)

The source half of the pair. Every track here is the pre-2000 record that a 2026 producer went back to — the raw material behind **[Remix Rave — 2026 Reworks](../remix-rave/remix-rave.md)**. Same fourteen numbers, so original **#8** (Robert Miles' *Children*, 1996) is the seed of rework **#8** (Steve Hill & Technikal's hard-trance cover).

Played in order it's a fast tour of dance music's spine, 1983 → 1999: New Order and Rufus & Chaka Khan open the set; Detroit and Belgium arrive in 1990 (808 State, Age Of Love); the vocal-house and Eurodance years fill the middle (Cajmere & Dajae, N-Trance, Livin' Joy, Gala); and it closes on the class of '99 — Fragma, Alice Deejay, Fatboy Slim — the newest sources the reworks reach back to.

**Method.** Each row links the Discogs **master release** (click "See all versions" there for any pressing), with the Artist and Track copied exactly as credited. The YouTube link is drawn from each release page's own community-attached videos where possible, and every video id was confirmed live before shipping. The Notes name the 2026 rework and link straight to its row in the sibling playlist.

## How the pairing works

- Numbers are shared across the two playlists: original **#N** ↔ rework **#N**. This list is sorted by the original's year; the reworks list is sorted by 2026 release date, so the two won't scroll in lockstep — follow the number.
- *Toca Me* (#12) is the 1999 instrumental whose riff, joined to Coco's "I Need A Miracle" (1997), became Fragma's *Toca's Miracle* — the thing the 2026 version actually reworks.
- Where a track's most famous dancefloor form is a 1990s remix rather than the first pressing, that's the cut featured (still pre-2000, still the version the rework leans on); the master link exposes every other version.
